The bathcoupling factors comes from the Gauss-Legendre quadratures' weights αi which are fixed values when have a certain number of the knots xi∈[−1,1].

In the gapped model, we have two bands. i.e. Valence band and Conduction band. The weights can be linearly transformated into a more general representation α~i.

α~i=length of band2⋅αi

  • Length of band: length of the valence band or conduction band.

  • ⋅2 : xi are sampled from [−1,1]​​ already with length 2. Dividing by 2 is normalisation from the Legendre polynomial's roots.

Density of State in Newns-Anderson Model

The most basic property of density of states is

∫ρDOS(ϵ)dϵ=N

where N stands for the number of states in our space. Just simply consider the energies are discretized as {ϵ1,⋯,ϵN}, we should find

(1)∫ρDOS(ϵ)dϵ=N=∑kN∫δ(ϵ−ϵk)dϵ

with the fact that

∫δ(ϵ−ϵk)dϵ=1for all k

By implying a stronger equality than (1), the definition of DOS is

(DOS)ρDOS(ϵ):=∑kδ(ϵ−ϵk).

Just remind that ρDOS​​ is defined as (DOS). Knowing (1) does not imply to (DOS). But (DOS) implies to (1).

Animation of the DOS

Hokseson made an animation of the electronic Hamiltonian with band centre at 5 eV. This DOS was scaled by the method mentioned above. The animation code can be found here.

This animation is generated by projected density of states ρprojected:

(PDOS)ρprojected=∑i|⟨ϕi|ϕprojected⟩|2δ(ϵ−ϵi).

The basic idea is projecting a function to one of its eigenstates ϕprojected. Any wavefunction can be represented by a linear combination of its eigenstates which we call it as superposition.

|Φ⟩=a1|ϕi⟩+a2|ϕ2⟩+⋯+an|ϕn⟩

where ai2 is the probability that |Φ⟩ occurs in state |ϕi⟩. Also, |ϕprojected⟩∈{|ϕi⟩}. When we want to project |Φ⟩ onto a eigenstate, it can be simply done by

⟨ϕprojected|Φ⟩=∑i⟨ϕprojected|ϕi⟩ai=aprojected

This comes from the fact that our Hamiltonian is symmetric resulting the orthonormal eigenstates. Apart from wavefunction, density of states is an another way to describe a quantum system. It relates to probability from its name "density" which is the square of the wavefunction. In terms of the projection in density of states, we basically square the wavefunction and replace the aprojected2 with δ(ϵ−ϵi) from |Φ⟩ and obtain (PDOS).

In our implementation, this projection can be done by setting the scaling factors of the Gaussian functions
